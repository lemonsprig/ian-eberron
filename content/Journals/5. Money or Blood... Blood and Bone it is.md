The moment the enormous two-headed ettin, Yadha-Rho, arrived in the square outside Drummond’s shop, the air turned tense. One head bellowed for Drummond, the other promised payment in money or blood. Stake attempted to speak with her, trying to convince her to return later. The party tried to reason, distract, and negotiate, but their words rolled off her thick hide. Stake ran upstairs and found Drummond cowering beneath his bed, too terrified to act.

Stake hauled Halas Drummond out from under his bed and threw him down the stairs. With Drummond lying at the bottom in a crumpled heap, the party tried to persuade him to reveal where he kept his money, or at least how long it would take him to get it. Drummond insisted he had none, explaining that the forge had been shut for weeks due to the strike and that he had only just paid the warforged to return to work. Saal, thinking practically, noted that businesses like Drummond’s would not keep piles of gold lying around anyway, instead relying on the Kundarak banking system for major payments.

Yadha-Rho’s patience finally snapped. She smashed through the door and demanded Drummond. Stake and Yadha-Rho then began an odd contest of pushing Drummond back and forth between them, the poor man too terrified even to scream.

Drummond fainted from terror when Yadha-Rho raised her club and announced it was leg breaking time. Stake grinned at her and said he would like to see that, and Yadha-Rho obliged without hesitation. She brought her club down with brutal force, shattering bone and kneecap.

Before anyone could react, Saal unleashed powerful magic that caused Yadha-Rho to see a giant cave spider dropping onto her heads and biting her. Yadha-Rho shrieked and clawed at her face, stumbling wildly around the forge. The rest of the party saw nothing on her, but her panic was real. Stake began smashing the floor with his hammer, attempting to kill any spiders he imagined might be there.

In her frenzy, Yadha-Rho ran to the furnace and plunged her heads into the hot coals in a desperate attempt to burn the spider away. Her skin blistered and her hair singed, but she continued to thrash. Two goblins who had come to support her took one look at the spectacle and fled immediately.

As she tore herself from the furnace, a fiery spider conjured by Eryx’s magic crawled from the burning pools and skittered across the floor. This was the final straw. Screaming and staggering, Yadha-Rho fled the building. Stake followed long enough to see her collapse in the street, unable to withstand her wounds. Once she lay still, he closed her eyes theatrically and, under the guise of compassion, lifted her coin purse. Two goblins watched from the shadows, saying nothing.

Inside the forge, they found Tresca hiding beneath a pile of coal. When she heard Yadha-Rho calling for Drummond earlier, she had buried herself among the lumps out of fear. Korruk examined Drummond and quickly concluded the leg was beyond saving. If he were to live, the limb would have to come off. They agreed he needed immediate healing.

Stake went upstairs again to look for gold in Drummond’s room but found nothing except the sealed Kundarak writ the party had originally given him as payment. He carefully opened the wax seal and examined the document. Inside was a promissory note to pay Halas Drummond two thousand gold from Provost Nigel Faurious. Stake quietly slipped the writ into his pack.

Using a table as a makeshift stretcher, Stake and Eryx carried Drummond while Korruk held the Burning Idol. They made their way to a House Jorasco clinic deeper in the Cogs. The building was circular with four branching halls. Inside, the stink of the Cogs vanished, replaced with the scent of flowers, incense, and the sound of gentle music.

A halfling healer rushed over, abandoning all pleasantries at the sight of Drummond’s mangled leg. He explained that the injury was severe, and without powerful magic, the chance of walking again was slim. Even with such magic, months of recovery would be required. When asked about cost, the party said they were simply delivering him to care. Tresca agreed to cover the initial treatment, and Drummond could decide the rest when he woke.

With Drummond finally in safe hands, the group decided to return to the Clifftop Guild and deliver the idol. They briefly debated taking another lift to avoid Gritz’s chatter, but ultimately chose to endure him in the hopes he knew something about the strange tower.

Gritz, excited as always, had never seen the tower but knew its history. It was not a mage’s tower, but the Tower of Aegix, a mighty hobgoblin warrior of old. Unfortunately, Gritz became less forthcoming when Stake commented that Aegix could not have been that great because he was dead. Gritz insisted he had died of old age, but it did little to soften Stake’s opinion. They did, however, learn that Aegix supposedly walked the planes and time itself.

Upon reaching the guild, Lhara greeted them. She was pleased to see the idol but surprised they had taken so long. After hearing an abbreviated explanation of their misadventures, she smiled knowingly and led them to her study.

There, she introduced their sponsor, Provost Nigel Faurious. Saal knew him well, and Nigel greeted him warmly, calling him m'boy. Nigel immediately set to work examining the idol from every angle, scribbling frantically in a large book and soon forgetting anyone else was in the room.

Stake interrupted, reminding Nigel that they had completed the job and should be paid. The room chilled noticeably as Lhara fixed him with an expression of pure disapproval. Nigel sensed the shift, clutched the idol, and excused himself quickly.

Lhara reprimanded Stake firmly, echoed by the rest of the party. After a moment her expression softened and she congratulated the others on a job well done. She poured glasses of brandy for everyone except Stake. Eryx declined his drink, and Stake immediately snatched the untouched glass and downed it. Saal found the liquor exceptional.

Lhara then produced four small pouches containing one hundred gold each. She handed them out, hesitating before tossing one to Stake, a pointed reminder of who was in charge. Stake appeared entirely unconcerned.

They asked about rooms, but Lhara said guild lodging was for those who had proven themselves worthy, once again giving Stake a pointed look. She directed them to the Feathered Harp instead. For shopping, she recommended the Dragon’s Hoard, which occasionally sold magical items obtained by other adventurers. As guild members they would receive a small discount. With that, she dismissed them to enjoy themselves until the next job.

Outside, Stake told the others about the writ he had taken from Drummond’s room. Saal and Eryx immediately advised leaving it alone. Angering Daask and the Boromar Clan was bad enough without adding financial crimes against House Kundarak.

At the Dragon’s Hoard, they were greeted by a wide-eyed gnomish child with blonde pigtails who immediately began shrieking for attention. She yelled, Dad, dad, customers Daaaad! until a gnome emerged from the back. When asked his name, he stuttered and begged them not to laugh before admitting his name was Bartholmew Sampson Jones. Stake laughed. Bart introduced his daughter Cynthia.

Korruk began browsing magical items when he noticed his reflection in a mirror wink at him. The mirror moved differently than he did and whispered inside his mind that he was strong, powerful, and that his blood was not weak. Bart explained it was a vanity mirror that told the viewer whatever they wanted to hear.

Bart then tried to sell the mirror outright, but Korruk explained he had no home of his own. Bart nodded sagely, vanished between two shelves, and returned a few minutes later dragging a large sack. Korruk examined it just as Bart presented the replacement item: a portable home, a simple tent.

Stake meanwhile attempted to sell the glowing rock given to him by Chandra Thane. Bart offered five gold, pointing out that he already had several. Stake instead bought a blue one for ten gold, then two pink ones Cynthia demonstrated by bouncing their lights between them. She slipped a small candy treat into Stake’s hand.

Korruk requested a shield. Bart vanished into the back room and returned with a tray holding a teacup, mugs, and a bowl of sugar cubes. With theatrical flair, he asked Korruk to help clear the cups, revealing the tray was actually a shield. After casting identify, Korruk confirmed it was a plus one shield. Bart asked one thousand gold for it, far beyond their means.

Saal inquired about scrolls. Cynthia returned with a box of paper scraps, receipts, torn sheets, and pages covered in childish drawings. Mixed among them were scrolls of detect magic, color spray, and magic missile. Bart offered them at a low price given the condition. Saal was ready to purchase them but realized he needed components. Korruk purchased color spray and magic missile and handed the former to Saal.
