With the tears of the Sindrial collected, Tresca informed the party that it would take a few hours for her to finish the enchantments and allow the potions they requested to properly brew. While they waited, the group debated what to do next. They discussed taking the Burning Idol back to the Clifftop Guild, returning to investigate the strange tower, or resting until the potions were ready.

Eryx asked Tresca about the presence of undead in the depths, but she could offer little beyond saying that it was not common, though not unheard of, for people to go missing in the lower reaches and for zombies or worse to be seen from time to time. When they mentioned the strange passage, she advised that places like that were best avoided.

Asked about the frozen tower, she admitted that she found it unsettling. It was strange, she said, and though she had worked near it for years, she often found herself forgetting its details soon after leaving. When pressed on why her curiosity had not led her to study it more closely, she told them simply that it was too big for her to understand.

The party considered returning to Clifftop but did not relish another lift ride with Gritz and his endless stories about goblin history. Still, it occurred to them that he might actually know something about the tower. Deciding it was too far to go for now, and needing rest, they sought a place to stay in the Cogs. The Red Hammer, while welcoming, had no beds for the living, only chairs and refreshments for warforged.

Stake suggested the Broken Anvil, about forty minutes’ walk from their current location. As they made their way there, the tunnels and streets grew more rundown, and the air took on an acrid sourness that lingered even above the normal stench of the Cogs.

Inside the Broken Anvil, the same smell filled the room. They were greeted by Cathy, a cantankerous old woman who barked at them to close the bloody door. The patrons fell silent for a moment, sizing up the newcomers, then returned to their drinks. One of them gave Stake a faint nod of recognition.

Food and beer were ordered, along with rooms for the night. Ale cost one copper, food another copper, and the rooms a silver each. Two rooms were taken: one for Eryx and Saal, the other for Stake and Korruk. The stew was thin and suspiciously rat-like, but the group was too tired to care.

As they ate, Korruk noticed that one patron had slipped out quietly, leaving behind his meal and a full mug. When he asked Cathy about it, she refused to speak without payment. With a sigh, Korruk passed her a silver. She told him the man’s name was Jorgen, a regular, but she did not know why he had left so suddenly.

The party retired for the night.

After only a few hours of sleep, Korruk and Stake were violently awakened as three men burst into their room, laying into them with leather-wrapped clubs. Eryx, roused by the commotion, left his trance to investigate and found two more men in the corridor. They told him to return to his room, that this was not his fight.

Korruk and Stake fought back, drawing weapons and fighting with lethal intent. Eryx ignored the warning and moved to assist, leaving Saal still asleep. When Saal finally awoke and opened his door, one of the thugs repeated the same message: go back to bed, not your fight. Saal cast Friends on the man and asked what was happening. His new companion hesitated, saying it was just a beatdown, payback for what Stake and Korruk had done to them.

The fight was brutal. Eryx struck one attacker with a blow that nearly severed his arm. The man surrendered but lived only moments before Stake cut him down. Seeing the tide turn and real weapons drawn, Saal’s new acquaintance panicked, calling them butchers before fleeing down the hall.

Another thug tried to escape, but Stake charged after him, goring him in the corridor and sending him tumbling down the stairs in a bloody heap. Entrails and blood splattered the walls as the remaining patrons below shouted in alarm and began to flee.

Stake and Korruk followed the sound of chaos downstairs, Stake stopping briefly to loot the bodies for coin. Below, Cathy and Korruk argued. She told them to gather their friends and get out. Stake tried to calm her, but she would not listen. When Korruk remarked that she ought to protect her patrons, Cathy snapped that she protected her inn, not fools who brought trouble to her door. The two left, and on the way out, Stake tore the Broken Anvil’s sign from the wall.

Eryx and Saal came down shortly after and tried speaking with Cathy themselves. Saal, as persuasive as ever, asked what had truly happened. Cathy said it had been meant as a simple beating by men from the Boromar Clan, payback for an earlier run-in with Stake and Korruk. It was supposed to be cracked ribs and bruises, but now the attackers were dead, butchered, and the pair had made powerful enemies. She wanted them all gone before the Boromar sent more.

Saal asked how he and Eryx could distance themselves, pointing out that their room was untouched. Cathy shook her head. They had been seen drinking with Stake and Korruk, so guilt by association was likely. After a moment, she offered one possibility: if the blood price was paid, the Boromar might let it go. Ten gold per body, forty in total.

Not wanting to be hunted by one of Sharn’s most dangerous crime syndicates, Saal reluctantly paid the full amount. Cathy promised to try, but could not guarantee anything. She still demanded that they leave.

Outside, bruised, exhausted, and in worse need of rest than before, they decided to return to the Red Hammer. The walk would take more than an hour in the opposite direction, and the complaints were many. Along the way, Stake stopped a goblin in the street, demanding to know where they could find a place to sleep. The goblin hesitated until Korruk pressed four gold into his hand. Suddenly all smiles, he introduced himself as Tolick and offered them space at his home.

He led them to a large, run-down building with dozens of locks on the front door. Korruk quickly noticed that none of the locks were actually attached. Inside, a crowd of goblins gathered around a firepit, something unidentifiable roasting unevenly over the flames, cooked in places, raw in others, and blackened in patches.

Tolick offered food, beer, and a place on the floor, even suggesting that if they wanted company, he could find them some goblin women. The party declined, but gratefully accepted the warmth and rest.

In the morning, Tolick bid them farewell, saying they were welcome any time and next time he would give them a good price, only three gold.

When they returned to the forge, Tresca greeted them and began bottling their potions. Stake waited outside and soon saw a massive, one-armed, two-headed creature stomp into the square. Slamming its club into the ground one head roared, “Drummond, you maggot!” The other followed, “It’s time to pay!” Then they alternated, “Money or blood!” “Blood or money!” before shouting together, “It’s time to pay, Drummond! Daask wants its due!”
