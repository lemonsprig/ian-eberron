# Starting Equipment

_Campaign-specific gear reflecting Eberron's magical technology and recent war._

## Standard Starting Gear

All characters begin with their normal class equipment plus the following campaign-specific items:

### Universal Starting Items

- **Traveler's Papers:** Official identification from your nation of origin
- **War Service Record:** If your character is 20+ years old (even non-combatants have records)
- **Regional Currency:** 2d4 additional gold pieces in your nation's coinage
- **Language Primer:** Small phrasebook for Common and one regional language

## Eberron-Specific Equipment

### Magical Technology Access

#### Lightning Rail Pass

**Cost:** 5 gp  
**Description:** A wooden or metal token enchanted with a permanent _arcane mark_. Grants passage on House Orien's lightning rail network for one month.

**Types Available:**

- **Third Class:** Hard seats, cargo areas (standard)
- **Second Class:** +10 gp, comfortable seating, meals included
- **First Class:** +25 gp, private compartments, luxury service

#### Speaking Stone Pair

**Cost:** 50 gp  
**Description:** Two matched crystals that allow communication over short distances (1 mile range). Powered by Eberron dragonshards.

**Usage:** Speak into one stone, holder of the matched stone hears your words. Popular for business communications and family connections.

### Dragonmarked House Services

#### House Service Voucher

**Value:** 20 gp credit  
**Description:** Prepaid services from any Dragonmarked House. Can be used for healing, transport, communication, or other House specialties.

**Restrictions:** Cannot be converted to cash, expires after one year.

#### Kundarak Bank Note

**Value:** 50 gp  
**Description:** A letter of credit backed by House Kundarak's banking network. Accepted anywhere the House operates.

**Benefits:** Safer than carrying coin, can be verified at any Kundarak facility.

### War Surplus Equipment

#### Military Kit

**Cost:** 15 gp  
**Description:** Surplus gear from the Last War, still in good condition.

**Contains:**

- Military-issue backpack (extra durable)
- Mess kit and water purification tablets
- 50 feet of hemp rope
- Grappling hook
- Tinderbox and 10 torches
- Bedroll and blanket
- 10 days of rations

#### Veteran's Gear

**Available to:** Characters with military backgrounds  
**Description:** Personal equipment retained from military service.

**Choose One:**

- **Officer's Kit:** Fine uniform, signet ring, maps of local area, command whistle
- **Scout's Kit:** Camouflaged cloak, spyglass, signal mirror, trail rations
- **Medic's Kit:** Healer's kit (enhanced), bandages, field surgery tools
- **Siege Engineer's Kit:** Hammer, crowbar, pitons, measuring cord

### Warforged-Specific Equipment

#### Warforged Integration Tools

**Cost:** 25 gp  
**Available to:** Warforged characters only  
**Description:** Tools for maintaining and upgrading warforged bodies.

**Contains:**

- Articulation oil (prevents joint stiffness)
- Polishing cloth and metal cleaner
- Small replacement parts
- Integration manual

#### Freedom Papers

**Cost:** Special (see description)  
**Available to:** Warforged characters only  
**Description:** Official documentation of your freedom under the Treaty of Thronehold.

**Note:** All player character warforged begin with these papers. Losing them creates significant legal complications.

### Magical Equipment

#### Eberron Dragonshard

**Cost:** 25 gp (least), 100 gp (lesser)  
**Description:** Small crystal that powers magical items and enhances spells.

**Uses:**

- Powers speaking stones and other devices
- Components for certain spells
- Artificer infusion focus

#### Everburning Torch

**Cost:** 110 gp  
**Description:** Torch with _continual flame_ effect, never burns out.

**Note:** More expensive than standard D&D due to wartime economy, but still available.

#### Feather Token

**Cost:** 50 gp each  
**Types:** Tree, bird, fan, anchor, whip

**Description:** Small magical items that activate when thrown, providing utility effects.

### Mundane but Eberron-Specific Items

#### Identification Papers

**Cost:** 2 gp (legitimate), 25 gp (forged)  
**Description:** Required for travel between nations, entry to major cities.

**Types:**

- Travel visa
- Work permit
- Military discharge papers
- House membership certificate

#### Regional Newspapers

**Cost:** 1 cp each  
**Examples:**

- _Sharn Inquisitive_ (Breland's major newspaper)
- _Aundair Arcane Review_ (magical research publication)
- _Thrane Theological Tribune_ (religious news)
- _Karrnath Military Dispatch_ (army communications)

#### Wartime Maps

**Cost:** 5 gp  
**Description:** Detailed maps showing battle sites, refugee camps, and current borders.

**Utility:** Valuable for navigation, historical context, and adventure planning.

## Character Background Equipment

### Dragonmarked Characters

Add **House Signet Ring** and **Letter of Introduction** to any House-affiliated character. These provide:

- Access to House facilities
- Credit for basic services
- Social recognition
- Minor legal protections

### War Veterans

Choose one piece of **War Memento:**

- Medal of valor
- Piece of battlefield debris
- Letter from fallen comrade
- Weapon from significant battle
- Map of strategic location

### Refugees (Especially Cyran)

Begin with **Refugee Documentation:**

- Identity papers from lost homeland
- List of missing family members
- Deed to property in the Mournland
- Personal memento from home
- Refugee camp identification

## Equipment Restrictions

### Magical Items

- No permanent magical items at character creation without DM approval
- Consumable magical items limited to common rarity
- Artificer starting equipment follows normal rules

### Military Equipment

- Warforged cannot begin with military-grade integrated weapons
- Siege weapons require special permits
- Magical weapons restricted to common enchantments

### Restricted Substances

- Dreamlily and other drugs controlled by local authorities
- Khyber dragonshards require special handling permits
- Undead-creation materials illegal in most nations

## Currency and Banking

### Starting Funds

Use standard class starting wealth, but consider:

- War veterans might have saved military pay
- House members enjoy better credit
- Refugees might have lost everything

### Coinage

Each nation mints its own currency, but all accept:

- **Galifar Standard:** Pre-war imperial currency (most trusted)
- **House Kundarak Notes:** Banking letters of credit
- **Precious Metals:** Gold and silver always valuable

### Exchange Rates

- Same-nation currency: 1:1 exchange
- Foreign currency: 5% exchange fee
- Kundarak notes: 2% service fee
- Precious metals: Market rate (varies)

## Equipment Acquisition

### House Services

Dragonmarked House facilities offer equipment rentals, repairs, and specialized services. Prices are standardized across the continent but availability varies by location.

### Black Market

Illegal or restricted items available through criminal contacts at 150-300% normal cost. Quality and authenticity not guaranteed.

### War Surplus

Many cities have surplus equipment dealers selling leftover military gear at reduced prices (75% of standard cost).

### Regional Specialties

Each nation produces certain equipment more efficiently:

- **Aundair:** Magical items and scholarly supplies
- **Breland:** Industrial tools and mechanical devices
- **Karrnath:** Military equipment and preserved goods
- **Thrane:** Religious items and healing supplies

_For standard equipment costs and descriptions, see **Player's Handbook** and **Eberron: Rising from the Last War**_
