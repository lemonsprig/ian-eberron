# Class Considerations

_How character classes fit into Eberron's unique magical and social landscape._

## Core Concepts

### Magic is Commonplace

In Eberron, magic serves as technology. Magical practitioners aren't mysterious hermits—they're professionals, researchers, and everyday workers. A wizard might work in a factory, a cleric could run a hospital, and a sorcerer might operate a taxi service.

### Religion Without Divine Proof

Divine magic works, but the gods remain silent. Clerics and paladins must grapple with faith without certainty, making their devotion more personal and their doctrine more flexible.

### Pulp Adventure Tone

Eberron embraces action-adventure storytelling. Characters are competent heroes capable of extraordinary feats, especially when using Action Points to enhance their abilities.

## Class-by-Class Considerations

### Artificer

**Perfect Fit for Eberron**

**Cultural Role:** Artificers represent Eberron's magical technology. They're engineers, inventors, and problem-solvers who blur the line between magic and mundane craftsmanship.

**House Connections:** Strong ties to House Cannith, though other Houses employ artificers for specialized needs.

**Warforged Artificers:** Particularly thematic—artificial beings who understand artificial creation.

**Specialization Notes:**

- **Alchemist:** Fits well with magical research and healing
- **Armorer:** Perfect for Last War veterans and House Deneith guards
- **Artillerist:** Reflects Eberron's magical warfare innovations
- **Battle Smith:** Ideal for beast handlers and construct creators

### Barbarian

**Uncommon in Civilized Areas**

**Cultural Context:** Most barbarians come from frontier regions—Eldeen Reaches, Talenta Plains, or Droaam. They're often seen as primitive by urban dwellers.

**Rage Mechanics:** Could represent berserker traditions, druidic fury, or even aberrant dragonmark manifestations.

**Primal Paths:**

- **Berserker:** Traditional rage warriors from tribal cultures, particularly common among Carrion Tribes
- **Wild Heart:** Perfect for Shifter characters or those with strong animal totems from Eldeen Reaches
- **World Tree:** Could represent druids touched by the great trees of the Eldeen Reaches or Towering Wood
- **Zealot:** Fits religious warriors of the Silver Flame or tribal shamans devoted to primal spirits

### Bard

**Information Brokers and Entertainers**

**House Connections:** Strong ties to House Phiarlan and House Thuranni, though the Houses maintain their split carefully.

**Social Role:** Bards serve as entertainers, diplomats, spies, and historians. They're welcome in courts and taverns alike.

**Colleges:**

- **Dance:** Perfect for House Phiarlan performers and cultural ambassadors
- **Glamour:** Fits fey-touched performers or House entertainers with connections to Thelanis
- **Lore:** Ideal for scholars and information gatherers at institutions like Morgrave University
- **Valor:** Warrior-poets who fought in the Last War, combining martial prowess with inspiration

### Cleric

**Faith Without Divine Certainty**

**The Challenge:** Clerics must choose their faith without divine confirmation. This creates rich roleplaying opportunities around doubt, conviction, and personal belief.

**Major Faiths:**

- **Sovereign Host:** Most common, flexible pantheon
- **Silver Flame:** Crusading church with political power
- **Blood of Vol:** Forbidden but practiced, especially in Karrnath
- **Dragon Below:** Dangerous cults worshipping alien entities

**Domains:**

- **Life:** Silver Flame templars, Host healers, and charitable clerics
- **Light:** Silver Flame purifiers and Dol Arrah worshippers
- **Trickery:** Olladra followers and some Traveler cultists
- **War:** Dol Dorn followers, military chaplains, and battle clerics

### Druid

**Guardians of Natural Balance**

**Cultural Role:** Particularly important in the Eldeen Reaches, where druids wield political power. They maintain ancient traditions predating civilized nations.

**The Gatekeepers:** Ancient orc druids who guard against aberrant threats from the Dragon Below.

**Circles:**

- **Land:** Traditional druids maintaining regional balance, especially powerful in the Eldeen Reaches
- **Moon:** Shifter druids embracing beast heritage and wild shape mastery
- **Sea:** Coastal druids along the Thunder Sea or those connected to water elementals
- **Stars:** Mystic druids who study celestial patterns and the Ring of Siberys, possibly connected to the Draconic Prophecy

### Fighter

**The Backbone of Civilization**

**War Veterans:** Many fighters are Last War veterans, bringing military experience and battlefield scars to their adventures.

**House Roles:** House Deneith employs many fighters as guards and mercenaries.

**Fighting Styles:**

- **Champion:** Straightforward soldiers and guards, the backbone of national armies
- **Battle Master:** Perfect for military tacticians and House Deneith officers
- **Eldritch Knight:** Combines martial and magical training, popular in Aundair's military
- **Psi Warrior:** Could represent Kalashtar martial traditions or aberrant mental powers

### Monk

**Unusual but Present**

**Cultural Context:** Monastic traditions exist but aren't widespread. They might represent:

- Aerenal meditation practices
- Silver Flame contemplative orders
- Kalashtar psychic disciplines
- Secret martial arts schools

**Monastic Traditions:**

- **Warrior of Mercy:** Fits Silver Flame healing orders or charitable monasteries
- **Warrior of Shadow:** Perfect for House Thuranni operatives or shadow monks
- **Warrior of the Elements:** Could represent elemental binding traditions or Seren Islands monasteries
- **Warrior of the Open Hand:** Traditional monastic training and disciplined martial arts

### Paladin

**Oaths Without Divine Mandate**

**The Challenge:** Like clerics, paladins must choose their cause without divine confirmation. Their power comes from conviction, not divine blessing.

**Sacred Oaths:**

- **Devotion:** Silver Flame templars and steadfast Host champions
- **Glory:** War heroes seeking to continue their legendary deeds in peacetime
- **Ancients:** Eldeen guardians protecting ancient groves and fey-touched warriors
- **Vengeance:** War veterans seeking justice for wartime atrocities or personal losses

### Ranger

**Frontier Guardians and Urban Hunters**

**Diverse Roles:** Rangers serve as wilderness guides, urban trackers, House Tharashk bounty hunters, and frontier guardians.

**Favored Enemies:** Consider Eberron-specific threats like aberrations (Dragon Below cultists), undead (Karrnathi necromancy), or constructs (rogue warforged).

**Ranger Archetypes:**

- **Beast Master:** Works well with House Vadalis connections and animal companions
- **Fey Wanderer:** Perfect for those touched by Thelanis or operating in the Eldeen Reaches
- **Gloom Stalker:** Ideal for Mournland explorers and those who hunt in darkness
- **Hunter:** Classic bounty hunter or frontier guardian specializing in specific prey

### Rogue

**Information, Infiltration, and Independence**

**Urban Specialists:** Rogues thrive in Eberron's cities, serving as spies, thieves, investigators, and information brokers.

**House Connections:** House Phiarlan and Thuranni employ rogues for espionage, while House Medani uses them for investigation.

**Roguish Archetypes:**

- **Arcane Trickster:** Combines stealth with magical utility, popular in House Cannith
- **Assassin:** House Thuranni operatives (use carefully)
- **Soulknife:** Kalashtar rogues using psychic blades or those touched by aberrant powers
- **Thief:** Classic criminals, burglars, and those who live by their wits

### Sorcerer

**Raw Magic and Bloodline Power**

**Dragonmark Connection:** Many sorcerers trace their power to dragonmark bloodlines, even if they don't manifest marks themselves.

**Sorcerous Origins:**

- **Draconic Bloodline:** Could represent dragonmark heritage or draconic influence from ancient times
- **Wild Magic:** Might reflect aberrant dragonmarks or Mournland exposure
- **Aberrant Mind:** Perfect for those touched by the Dragon Below or daelkyr influence
- **Clockwork Soul:** Could represent artificial magical enhancement or connection to Modron-like entities

### Warlock

**Dangerous Pacts and Forbidden Knowledge**

**Otherworldly Patrons:**

- **Archfey:** Fey courts from Thelanis plane, offering beauty and wonder at a price
- **Celestial:** Possibly connected to the Silver Flame or couatl spirits
- **Fiend:** Demons from Khyber or rakshasa overlords seeking to reclaim power
- **Great Old One:** Daelkyr or other aberrant entities from the Dragon Below

**Social Stigma:** Warlocks face suspicion, especially in Thrane where their power source might be considered heretical.

### Wizard

**Magical Scholars and Researchers**

**Academic Tradition:** Wizards represent Eberron's scholarly approach to magic. They study at universities, work in laboratories, and publish research.

**House Connections:** Many Houses employ wizards—Cannith for artifice, Lyrandar for weather magic, Sivis for communication spells.

**Arcane Traditions:**

- **Abjurer:** Popular in House Kundarak for protective magic and security
- **Diviner:** Valuable to House Medani investigators and intelligence gatherers
- **Evoker:** Military applications, war magic, and destructive research
- **Illusionist:** Perfect for House Phiarlan entertainers and spies

## Eberron-Specific Restrictions

### Blood of Vol Practitioners

Characters with obvious ties to the Blood of Vol face persecution in most nations, especially Thrane. This applies to:

- Death domain clerics (not available in 2024 core rules)
- Necromancy wizards
- Undead warlocks (not available in 2024 core rules)

**Playing These Classes:** Requires careful consideration of social implications and regional reactions.

### Aberrant Magic Users

Characters with aberrant dragonmarks or Dragon Below connections face fear and suspicion:

- Great Old One warlocks
- Aberrant Mind sorcerers
- Certain monk traditions (Warrior of Shadow if tied to aberrant powers)

### Regional Considerations

**In Thrane:**

- Divine casters face theological scrutiny
- Warlocks and necromancers risk inquisition
- Silver Flame templars hold authority

**In Karrnath:**

- Necromancy is more accepted
- Military backgrounds are respected
- Blood of Vol has underground presence

**In Aundair:**

- Wizards and artificers receive academic respect
- Magical research is encouraged
- Arcane traditions dominate

**In Breland:**

- Diversity is celebrated
- Innovation is valued
- Democratic ideals influence class roles

## Multiclassing Considerations

**Artificer/Wizard:** Perfect for House Cannith researchers **Fighter/War Cleric:** Military chaplains and templar knights **Rogue/Bard:** House Phiarlan agents and information brokers **Ranger/Druid:** Eldeen Reaches guardians and Gatekeeper initiates **Sorcerer/Warlock:** Dangerous combination, often aberrant-marked

## Character Creation Tips

**Consider Your Origin:** Where did you learn your abilities? A wizard from Arcanix differs greatly from one who studied in Sharn's alleys.

**Think About the War:** Characters over 20 likely experienced the Last War. How did it shape their class development?

**Embrace the Tone:** Eberron supports competent, proactive heroes. Don't be afraid to be effective and dramatic.

**Social Integration:** Consider how your class fits into Eberron's society. Are you common, unusual, or feared?
