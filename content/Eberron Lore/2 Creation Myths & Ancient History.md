# Creation Myths & Ancient History

_Before kings and crowns, before wars and nations—the deep history that shaped the world._

## The Creation of All Things

_"In the beginning, there were three dragons, and their conflict shaped everything that is."_

**The Progenitor Dragons:** Long before mortals walked Eberron, three great dragons embodied the fundamental forces of existence. Their eternal struggle created the world and continues to influence it today.

**Siberys, the Dragon Above:** The golden dragon of the skies became the ring of dragonshards that circles the world. Siberys represents divine power, inspiration, and the heights of achievement. The rare golden dragonshards that sometimes fall from the ring carry fragments of this progenitor's essence.

**Eberron, the Dragon Between:** The dragon of nature and balance became the world itself—the continents, oceans, and all living things. Eberron represents life, growth, and the material world. The blue dragonshards found in the earth are manifestations of this dragon's power.

**Khyber, the Dragon Below:** The shadow dragon was bound beneath the world, becoming the underworld of tunnels, caverns, and dark places. Khyber represents corruption, hidden knowledge, and dangerous power. The red dragonshards found in deep places carry echoes of this imprisoned force.

**The Draconic Prophecy:** From their cosmic struggle emerged the Prophecy—a vast pattern of fate and destiny written in dragonmarks, celestial movements, and world events. True dragons spend their long lives studying this Prophecy, seeking to understand and influence the world's destiny.

## The Age of Dragons

_"When dragons ruled, mortals cowered in caves and called it wisdom."_

**The First Civilization:** Dragons were Eberron's first intelligent inhabitants, establishing a vast civilization while other races remained primitive. Their cities floated in the sky, their magic reshaped continents, and their knowledge encompassed secrets that mortals still struggle to comprehend.

**The Dragon-Fiend Wars:** Ancient dragons battled the lords of the demon realm in conflicts that scarred the world. These wars taught dragons the arts of binding outsiders—knowledge that would later enable innovations like elemental binding and the creation of the warforged.

**The Couatl Sacrifice:** When fiends threatened to corrupt the world entirely, the noble couatl—feathered serpents allied with the dragons—made the ultimate sacrifice. They gave their lives to create the Silver Flame, a force of pure good that burns away evil. This event established one of Khorvaire's major religions.

**Dragon Withdrawal:** As humanoid races grew in number and power, most dragons retreated to remote places like Argonnessen, the dragon continent. They remain active but prefer to work through agents and prophecy rather than direct rule.

## The Giant Empire of Xen'drik

_"They built wonders that put our greatest cities to shame, then destroyed it all in their pride."_

**Masters of Magic:** Forty thousand years ago, giants ruled a continent-spanning empire in Xen'drik. Their magical achievements surpassed anything seen since—floating cities, continent-shaping rituals, and artifacts of incredible power.

**The Elven Slaves:** Giants enslaved the ancestors of modern elves, forcing them to work in mines, fields, and workshops. These elves learned giant magic while dreaming of freedom, knowledge that would serve them well after their escape.

**The Quori Connection:** Giant magic drew power from the plane of dreams, forming pacts with the quori—beings of living thought and nightmare. This alliance granted them incredible psychic abilities but also exposed them to corruption.

**The Great Rebellion:** Elven slaves rose against their masters in a revolt that consumed the continent. The rebellion coincided with the giants' war against the quori, stretching the empire beyond its breaking point.

**Divine Intervention:** As the giant empire teetered on collapse, their desperate experiments with planar magic threatened to destroy the world itself. The dragons of Argonnessen intervened, raining devastation on Xen'drik that shattered the continent and reduced giant civilization to scattered tribes.

**The Elven Exodus:** Surviving elves fled Xen'drik in a great migration, eventually settling in Aerenal where they developed their own unique culture centered on death-defying magic and ancestor worship.

## The Goblinoid Dhakaani Empire

_"Before human kingdoms, goblin armies marched in perfect formation across a united Khorvaire."_

**The First Khorvaire Empire:** Six thousand years ago, when humans were still nomadic tribes, the Dhakaani Empire united most of Khorvaire under goblin rule. Their civilization emphasized military excellence, engineering prowess, and rigid social hierarchy.

**Goblin Achievements:** The Dhakaani built the first great roads across Khorvaire, established trade networks that connected distant regions, and created a legal system that influenced later human kingdoms. Many ruins found throughout Khorvaire are remnants of their empire.

**The Perfect Army:** Dhakaani military organization was legendary. Their legions moved with precision unknown in later human armies, combining tactics, discipline, and magical support to conquer and hold vast territories.

**The Daelkyr Invasion:** The empire's downfall came from the depths of Khyber. The daelkyr—alien beings of madness and transformation—invaded the material plane nine thousand years ago, bringing aberrations and insanity with them.

**Madness and Mutation:** The daelkyr didn't just conquer; they corrupted. Their touch transformed landscapes into twisted wastelands, mutated creatures into aberrant horrors, and drove entire populations insane. The Dhakaani legions, for all their discipline, couldn't fight an enemy that warped reality itself.

**The Gatekeepers' Stand:** Orc druids called the Gatekeepers allied with the Dhakaani against the common threat. Using primal magic and ancient binding rituals, they helped seal the daelkyr beneath Khyber, but the cost was enormous—both the orc and goblinoid civilizations never fully recovered.

**The Scattered Clans:** The empire's collapse left goblinoid clans scattered across Khorvaire. Most forgot their ancient glory, becoming bandits and raiders. Only recently have some, like those in Darguun, begun reclaiming their heritage.

## The Human Arrival

_"We came as refugees and barbarians, but we learned quickly from those who came before."_

**The Western Migration:** Humans arrived in Khorvaire roughly three thousand years ago, fleeing some unknown catastrophe in Sarlona. They came not as conquerors but as desperate refugees seeking any safe haven.

**Learning from Ruins:** Early human tribes settled among the ruins of previous civilizations, learning from goblinoid architecture, elvish magic, and draconic lore. This inherited knowledge accelerated their development far beyond what they might have achieved alone.

**The Tribal Period:** For nearly two millennia, humans remained organized in competing tribes and small kingdoms. They gradually spread across Khorvaire, adapting to different environments and absorbing influences from other races.

**The Five Kingdoms:** Eventually, five great human kingdoms emerged in different regions of Khorvaire: Thrane in the east, Aundair in the northwest, Breland in the west, Karrnath in the northeast, and Cyre in the center. Each developed its own culture while maintaining trade and diplomatic relations.

**Dragonmark Emergence:** During this period, dragonmarks began manifesting in certain human bloodlines, granting magical abilities that transcended tribal and kingdom boundaries. These marked families formed the first dragonmarked houses, establishing the foundations of their future power.

## The Unification Wars

_"Galifar saw what others missed—that divided, we were vulnerable, but united, we could be unstoppable."_

**The Conqueror King:** A thousand years ago, Galifar I of Karrnath launched campaigns to unite all of Khorvaire under one crown. Unlike previous conquerors, he combined military might with political wisdom, marriage alliances, and respect for local customs.

**The Long Campaign:** Galifar's unification took decades and cost countless lives, but it was driven by vision rather than mere ambition. He believed that only a united Khorvaire could defend against the threats that had destroyed previous civilizations.

**The Five Nations:** Rather than crush the conquered kingdoms, Galifar incorporated them as integral parts of his empire. Each retained its cultural identity while contributing to the greater whole—Karrnath's military expertise, Aundair's magical knowledge, Breland's industrial innovation, Thrane's spiritual strength, and Cyre's artistic achievements.

**The Dragonmarked Alliance:** Galifar recognized the dragonmarked houses' growing power and made them partners rather than subjects. The Korth Edicts established their privileges and responsibilities, creating a relationship that benefited both crown and houses.

**The Golden Age:** For nearly nine centuries, the Kingdom of Galifar brought unprecedented peace and prosperity to Khorvaire. Trade flourished, magical innovation accelerated, and threats from other continents were successfully repelled.

## The Ancient Threats

_"The past is not dead—it sleeps beneath our feet, waiting for the right moment to wake."_

**The Bound Daelkyr:** Though sealed beneath Khyber, the daelkyr remain active. Their influence seeps upward through cults, aberrant dragonmarks, and twisted creatures that emerge from deep places. The Gatekeepers maintain their ancient vigil, but they grow fewer with each generation.

**The Dragon Prophecy:** True dragons continue to study the Draconic Prophecy, intervening in world events when they perceive threats to the cosmic order. Their involvement is rarely obvious, but their influence shapes history in subtle ways.

**The Quori Threat:** The quori, beings of living dream, were banished from the material plane during the giant empire's fall. They now work through the Dreaming Dark, a network of agents and cultists seeking to return to the waking world.

**Ancient Artifacts:** Ruins across Khorvaire contain artifacts of incredible power—giant eldritch machines, draconic binding stones, and Dhakaani war engines. Most are safely buried or warded, but adventurers and treasure hunters occasionally uncover items that were sealed away for good reason.

**The Mournland Mystery:** Cyre's destruction echoes patterns from ancient catastrophes. Some scholars worry that the same forces that destroyed the giant empire might be stirring again, though the exact nature of the threat remains unclear.

## Legacy of the Ages

**Cultural Fusion:** Modern Khorvaire culture blends influences from all previous civilizations—human ambition, goblinoid organization, elven artistry, and draconic wisdom create a unique synthesis found nowhere else.

**Inherited Knowledge:** Much of Eberron's magical technology builds on ancient foundations. Elemental binding descends from draconic techniques, architectural methods trace back to Dhakaani engineering, and even the creation of warforged incorporated secrets learned from giant artifice.

**Cyclic History:** Eberron's history follows patterns of rise and fall, innovation and destruction. Each civilization reaches great heights before some catastrophe brings it low, yet something always survives to seed the next age.

**The Eternal Struggle:** The conflict between the Progenitor Dragons continues to influence world events. Order versus chaos, creation versus destruction, revelation versus hidden knowledge—these tensions drive both personal conflicts and world-shaping events.

## For Player Characters

**Cultural Context:** Understanding ancient history helps explain why certain races, regions, and ruins exist. Goblinoids remember ancient glory, elves carry trauma from slavery, and orcs maintain ancient druidic traditions.

**Adventure Seeds:** Ancient ruins, lost artifacts, sleeping threats, and prophetic signs all stem from deep history. What adventurers do with this knowledge can shape the future.

**Racial Heritage:** Characters from older races (elves, orcs, goblins) might have cultural memories or family stories connected to ancient events.

**Religious Understanding:** Creation myths explain the fundamental forces of good and evil, the nature of the planes, and why dragonmarks matter beyond their practical applications.

**Prophetic Significance:** The Draconic Prophecy suggests that current events echo ancient patterns. Player characters might unwittingly fulfill ancient predictions or work to prevent catastrophic repetitions.
