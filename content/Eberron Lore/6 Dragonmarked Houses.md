# The Dragonmarked Houses

_Blood, business, and magic intertwined—the true power behind Khorvaire's throne._

## The Mark of Power

In the aftermath of Galifar's collapse, a new aristocracy emerged from the ashes—one based not on royal bloodlines or military conquest, but on the mysterious dragonmarks that manifest on certain individuals. These magical sigils grant their bearers extraordinary abilities, and the families that carry them have transformed from merchant guilds into continental superpowers.

The dragonmarked houses wield influence that transcends national borders. While kings and queens rule their respective nations, the houses control the infrastructure that keeps civilization running: transportation, communication, banking, and healing. They are the unseen threads that bind Khorvaire together, and the invisible hands that could tear it apart.

## The Nature of Dragonmarks

_"The mark chooses the bearer, not the other way around."_

**Hereditary Magic:** Dragonmarks appear only in specific bloodlines, manifesting as intricate patterns that resemble dragon scales. They typically emerge during adolescence or times of great stress, permanently marking their bearers as part of something greater than themselves.

**Growing Power:** Dragonmarks evolve through three stages: least, lesser, and greater. Most marked individuals never progress beyond the least mark, while greater marks are incredibly rare—perhaps one in ten thousand. The legendary Siberys marks are so rare that their very existence is debated.

**Exclusive Abilities:** Each house's dragonmark grants access to specific magical abilities that cannot be replicated through study or training. This biological monopoly forms the foundation of their economic power.

**The Unmarked:** Not every member of a dragonmarked house bears the mark. These "unmarked" family members often serve in leadership roles, freed from the obligations and restrictions that come with the mark itself.

## The Thirteen Houses

### House Cannith: The Forge of Innovation

**Mark of Making** | **Race:** Human | **Guild:** Tinkers Guild

_"We shape the world through will and artifice."_

**The Builders of Tomorrow:** House Cannith stands at the forefront of magical innovation. Their artificers created the warforged, pioneered mass production of magical items, and continue pushing the boundaries of what magic can achieve.

**The Shattered House:** The Last War devastated Cannith more than any other house. The destruction of their creation forges in Cyre eliminated their ability to create new warforged, while internal disputes split the house into three competing factions:

- **Cannith South** (Merrix d'Cannith): Based in Sharn, focuses on warforged rights and new creation techniques
- **Cannith West** (Jorlanna d'Cannith): Operates from Fairhaven, emphasizes profitable innovation
- **Cannith East** (Zorlan d'Cannith): Headquartered in Korth, pursues military applications

**Services:** Magical item creation, construct repair, architectural marvels, weapon and armor enhancement.

### House Deneith: The Blade Bearers

**Mark of Sentinel** | **Race:** Human | **Guild:** Blademarks Guild

_"Protection is not just our business—it is our sacred trust."_

**The Mercenary House:** Deneith transformed from a guild of bodyguards into the continent's premier military contractor. Their Blademarks Guild provides everything from personal protection to entire mercenary armies.

**Military Tradition:** The house maintains strict military discipline and codes of honor. Their reputation for reliability makes them indispensable to merchants, nobles, and nations alike.

**The Sentinel Marshals:** Elite agents who enforce the Korth Edicts—international laws governing the dragonmarked houses. They have authority to operate across national borders in pursuit of justice.

**Services:** Personal protection, mercenary forces, investigation services, security consulting, and law enforcement.

### House Ghallanda: The Hosts with the Most

**Mark of Hospitality** | **Race:** Halfling | **Guild:** Hostelers Guild

_"Every stranger is a friend we haven't met yet."_

**Masters of Comfort:** Ghallanda has elevated hospitality to an art form. Their inns provide not just food and shelter, but experiences that create lifelong memories and customer loyalty.

**The Golden Dragon Inn Chain:** Their flagship establishments offer consistent quality across the continent. The mark's magic ensures perfect meals, comfortable rest, and an almost supernatural ability to make guests feel welcome.

**Cultural Ambassadors:** More than any other house, Ghallanda bridges cultural divides. Their halfling sensibilities emphasize community, comfort, and the belief that good food and drink can solve most problems.

**Services:** Inns and taverns, catering, event planning, food preservation, and cultural mediation.

### House Jorasco: The Healing Touch

**Mark of Healing** | **Race:** Halfling | **Guild:** Healers Guild

_"Life is precious, and we are its guardians."_

**Medical Monopoly:** Jorasco controls magical healing across Khorvaire. Their houses of healing provide services ranging from minor injury treatment to resurrection magic—for those who can afford it.

**The Price of Life:** The house maintains strict pricing for their services, arguing that undervaluing healing devalues life itself. This policy saves countless lives while also generating enormous profits.

**Research and Development:** Jorasco healers constantly seek new applications for their abilities. They study diseases, develop new treatments, and explore the boundaries between life and death.

**Services:** Magical healing, disease treatment, resurrection services, medical research, and health consulting.

### House Kundarak: The Vault Keepers

**Mark of Warding** | **Race:** Dwarf | **Guild:** Banking Guild

_"Your treasures are safer with us than in your own hands."_

**Financial Empire:** Kundarak transformed from a clan of locksmiths into Khorvaire's central banking system. Their vaults are considered absolutely secure, their letters of credit accepted everywhere.

**Magical Security:** The Mark of Warding creates protections that few can breach. Kundarak vaults use multiple layers of magical and mundane security, making them legendary for their impregnability.

**Economic Influence:** By controlling banking and currency exchange, Kundarak wields enormous influence over trade and politics. Kings may rule nations, but Kundarak holds their purse strings.

**Services:** Banking, secure storage, magical locks and wards, insurance services, and financial consulting.

### House Lyrandar: Riders of Wind and Wave

**Mark of Storm** | **Race:** Half-Elf | **Guild:** Windwrights Guild

_"We command the elements themselves."_

**Masters of Transportation:** Lyrandar's control over wind and weather makes them indispensable for both airship and sea travel. Their elemental galleons and airships move goods and passengers faster than any alternative.

**Weather Wardens:** Beyond transportation, the house provides weather prediction and limited weather control. Farmers pay for favorable conditions, while cities hire them to prevent destructive storms.

**Elemental Binding:** Lyrandar artificers specialize in binding air and water elementals. Their techniques power not just vehicles, but also urban infrastructure like Sharn's floating towers.

**Services:** Airship and ship transportation, weather prediction and control, elemental binding, and maritime insurance.

### House Medani: The Warning Guild

**Mark of Detection** | **Race:** Half-Elf | **Guild:** Warning Guild

_"Knowledge is the greatest protection."_

**Information Brokers:** Medani's ability to detect lies, locate objects, and sense danger makes them invaluable for investigation and intelligence work. They know secrets that could topple governments.

**Bodyguard Services:** Unlike Deneith's martial protection, Medani offers subtle security through foresight and information. They prevent problems before they occur rather than fighting them afterward.

**The Neutral Ground:** Medani maintains strict neutrality in political conflicts, selling their services to all sides while keeping each client's secrets sacred. This reputation for discretion is their most valuable asset.

**Services:** Investigation, lie detection, background checks, early warning systems, and information brokering.

### House Orien: The Highway Masters

**Mark of Passage** | **Race:** Human | **Guild:** Transportation Guild

_"All roads lead through us."_

**The Lightning Rail Network:** Orien's greatest achievement is the lightning rail system that connects major cities across Khorvaire. These elemental-powered trains revolutionized commerce and travel.

**Courier Services:** Before lightning rails, Orien built their reputation on reliable message and package delivery. Their couriers still handle sensitive items that require personal attention.

**Teleportation Circles:** The house maintains teleportation networks for urgent travel, though the expense limits this service to the wealthy and desperate.

**Services:** Lightning rail transportation, courier services, teleportation, shipping, and logistics coordination.

### House Phiarlan: The Shadow Players

**Mark of Shadow** | **Race:** Elf | **Guild:** Entertainers and Artisans Guild

_"All the world's a stage, and we write the script."_

**Entertainment Empire:** Phiarlan presents itself as the continent's premier entertainment house, providing everything from tavern musicians to grand theatrical productions. Their artists are welcomed in every court and common room.

**The Hidden Truth:** Behind the masks and music lies one of Khorvaire's most sophisticated spy networks. Phiarlan entertainers gather information while they perform, trading secrets as easily as songs.

**Cultural Influence:** Through entertainment, the house shapes public opinion and cultural trends. A Phiarlan production can make or break reputations, influence elections, or start social movements.

**Services:** Entertainment, artistic training, cultural events, information gathering, and subtle influence operations.

### House Sivis: The Speakers of Truth

**Mark of Scribing** | **Race:** Gnome | **Guild:** Speakers Guild

_"Words have power, and we choose them carefully."_

**Communication Network:** Sivis operates the speaking stone networks that enable instant communication across continents. Their message stations are as common as inns in major cities.

**Legal Services:** The house provides translation, document authentication, and notarization services. Their magical verification makes Sivis documents nearly impossible to forge.

**Information Management:** Beyond simple communication, Sivis manages vast databases of information. They know who speaks to whom, when, and sometimes what they discuss.

**Services:** Magical communication, translation, document authentication, legal services, and information storage.

### House Tharashk: The Finders

**Mark of Finding** | **Race:** Human/Half-Orc | **Guild:** Finders Guild

_"What is lost can be found, for the right price."_

**Dragonshard Prospectors:** Tharashk built their fortune finding dragonshard deposits. Their prospectors brave dangerous wilderness to locate the crystal formations that power magical civilization.

**Bounty Hunters:** The house also finds people—escaped criminals, missing persons, or anyone else who doesn't want to be found. Their inquisitors are legendary for their persistence.

**Urban Operations:** In cities like Sharn, Tharashk operates as both security force and criminal organization, managing the balance between order and profitable chaos.

**Services:** Dragonshard prospecting, bounty hunting, investigation, security services, and urban "management."

### House Thuranni: The Shadow's Edge

**Mark of Shadow** | **Race:** Elf | **Guild:** Shadow Network

_"We are the darkness that shapes the light."_

**The Shadow Schism:** Thuranni split from Phiarlan following the Last War, taking the spy network's deadliest members. Where Phiarlan uses subtle influence, Thuranni employs direct action.

**Assassination Services:** The house provides elimination services to those who can afford their prices and meet their standards. They maintain codes of honor even in the business of death.

**Entertainment Front:** Like their Phiarlan cousins, Thuranni maintains legitimate entertainment businesses. Their artists tend toward darker themes—tragedy, horror, and the beauty found in shadows.

**Services:** Assassination, sabotage, intelligence operations, specialized entertainment, and shadow magic applications.

### House Vadalis: The Beast Tamers

**Mark of Handling** | **Race:** Human | **Guild:** Handlers Guild

_"Every creature has its place in the natural order."_

**Magebred Animals:** Vadalis created the magical breeding techniques that produce superior animals. Their magebred horses are faster and stronger, their hunting dogs more intelligent and loyal.

**Transportation Services:** Before lightning rails and airships, Vadalis mounts carried messages and passengers across Khorvaire. They still provide specialized transportation for difficult terrain.

**Agricultural Innovation:** The house works with farmers to improve livestock and develop new breeding techniques. Their innovations help feed the continent's growing population.

**Services:** Magebred animals, transportation, agricultural consulting, animal training, and veterinary services.

## The Korth Edicts

_"Even dragons must follow some rules."_

**International Law:** The Korth Edicts, negotiated after the War of the Mark, establish the legal framework governing dragonmarked house operations. These treaties prevent the houses from forming their own armies or claiming sovereign territory.

**Key Restrictions:**

- Houses cannot maintain standing armies beyond security forces
- Houses cannot claim political sovereignty over territory
- Houses must allow competitors limited access to their services
- Houses cannot deny services based on nationality during peacetime

**Enforcement:** The Sentinel Marshals of House Deneith enforce the edicts, though their effectiveness depends on house cooperation and political pressure.

## Economic Power

_"Kingdoms rise and fall, but the houses endure."_

**Continental Monopolies:** Each house controls essential services that span national borders. This economic power often exceeds that of individual governments.

**Political Influence:** Through economic leverage rather than direct rule, the houses shape policy across Khorvaire. A house's displeasure can cripple a nation's economy.

**Neutral Ground:** The houses officially maintain neutrality in national conflicts, but their services often determine which side has strategic advantages.

**Innovation Investment:** Houses pour enormous resources into research and development, driving magical innovation faster than any government or academic institution.

## Internal Politics

_"Blood may be thicker than water, but gold is thicker than blood."_

**Family Dynamics:** Each house grapples with internal tensions between generations, marked and unmarked members, and competing business philosophies.

**Succession Struggles:** House leadership doesn't always pass to the most capable or most marked individual. Politics, alliances, and accidents all influence who rises to power.

**Branch Competition:** Many houses operate semi-independent branches in different regions, leading to internal competition that sometimes benefits outsiders.

**Reform Movements:** Younger house members sometimes challenge traditional practices, seeking to modernize operations or address ethical concerns.

## The Future of the Houses

**Technological Evolution:** As magical technology advances, houses must adapt their services or risk obsolescence.

**Social Pressure:** Growing awareness of elemental rights, economic inequality, and house monopolies creates pressure for reform.

**International Relations:** The houses' power makes them both essential partners and potential threats to national governments.

**New Challenges:** Emerging threats like the Dreaming Dark, aberrant dragonmarks, and magical disasters require coordinated house responses.

## For Player Characters

**House Connections:** Characters with dragonmarks gain access to house resources and responsibilities. Unmarked characters might work for houses or compete against them.

**Economic Reality:** House services are expensive but reliable. Characters must balance cost against convenience and security.

**Political Neutrality:** The houses' neutral stance creates opportunities for characters to work across national boundaries.

**Adventure Hooks:** House politics, economic espionage, internal conflicts, and moral dilemmas provide countless adventure opportunities.

**Social Status:** Dragonmarked characters enjoy respect and suspicion in equal measure, while house employment provides stability and advancement opportunities.
