# The Five Nations

_From Galifar's ashes, five realms rose—each shaped by war, defined by ambition._

## The Empire That Was

Once, all of Khorvaire was united under the crown of Galifar. King Galifar I forged his empire through conquest and marriage, creating a realm that lasted nearly nine centuries. Each region developed its own character while serving the whole: Aundair's magical academies, Breland's industry, Cyre's arts, Karrnath's military, and Thrane's faith.

The Last War shattered this unity forever. When the Treaty of Thronehold was signed, the dream of reunification died with it. What emerged instead were five independent nations, each convinced of its own righteousness and suspicious of its neighbors.

## Aundair: The Realm of Wonder

_"Magic is not just our tool—it is our very soul."_

**The Magical Kingdom:** Aundair embraces arcane power like no other nation. Flying towers drift above its cities, elemental airships patrol its skies, and magic infuses daily life from farming to warfare.

**Queen Aurala's Vision:** The young queen dreams of magical supremacy, funding ambitious experiments while her floating city of Arcanix leads the world in mystical research.

**The Eldeen Problem:** Aundair lost its western territories when the Eldeen Reaches declared independence, backed by Breland. The wound still festers, with Aundairian loyalists in the Reaches dreaming of reunion.

**Cultural Identity:** Sophisticated, artistic, and occasionally arrogant. Aundairians see themselves as civilization's pinnacle, guided by reason and enlightened by magic.

## Breland: The Crown of Industry

_"Progress through innovation, strength through unity."_

**The Democratic Experiment:** Alone among the Five Nations, Breland embraces elected representation. King Boranel rules, but Parliament holds real power, and common citizens have voices their ancestors never imagined.

**Industrial Might:** Breland's factories and foundries powered the war effort and now drive peacetime prosperity. Sharn, the City of Towers, stands as the continent's greatest metropolis.

**The Melting Pot:** More diverse than its neighbors, Breland welcomes refugees, immigrants, and anyone willing to work hard. This openness breeds both strength and internal tension.

**Cultural Identity:** Pragmatic, ambitious, and surprisingly idealistic. Brelanders believe in earning their place rather than inheriting it, though old noble families still wield considerable influence.

## Karrnath: The Undying Kingdom

_"Death is just another battlefield to master."_

**The Necromantic Solution:** Desperate during the war's darkest years, Karrnath turned to the undead. Skeleton soldiers and zombie laborers still serve openly, making visitors deeply uncomfortable.

**King Kaius III's Rule:** The young king publicly renounced the Blood of Vol's influence and seeks to modernize his realm, but old habits die hard in a kingdom that literally won't let its dead rest.

**Military Tradition:** Karrnath produces the continent's finest soldiers. Their disciplined legions remain formidable even without undead reinforcement, and military service shapes the national character.

**Cultural Identity:** Stoic, disciplined, and pragmatically ruthless. Karrnathi accept necessary sacrifices others won't, whether in war, politics, or dealing with forces beyond death.

## Thrane: The Pure Flame

_"Let the Silver Flame light the way to righteousness."_

**The Theocracy:** When Queen Diani died childless, the Church of the Silver Flame claimed divine mandate to rule. Keeper of the Flame Jaela Daran, despite her youth, wields both spiritual and temporal authority.

**The Purification Doctrine:** Thrane sees itself as Khorvaire's moral guardian, sworn to burn away corruption wherever it lurks. This righteousness makes them both respected and feared.

**The Templar Orders:** Elite warriors serve the Flame's cause, hunting undead, demons, and other supernatural threats. Their dedication is unquestioned, their methods sometimes extreme.

**Cultural Identity:** Devout, zealous, and unflinchingly righteous. Thranes believe they serve a higher purpose, though neighbors sometimes question whether that purpose serves the greater good.

## The Mournland: The Wound That Won't Heal

_"Here lies Cyre, who dreamed of peace and found only silence."_

**The Lost Nation:** Where Cyre once stood, only twisted wasteland remains. The Mournland serves as a constant reminder of war's ultimate price and magic's potential for catastrophe.

**The Living Graveyard:** Despite its apparent desolation, the Mournland teems with unnatural life. Undead wander its paths, warforged seek purpose among the ruins, and stranger things emerge from the gray mists.

**Political Void:** Technically, Prince Oargev still claims Cyre's crown, but ruling a dead land from New Cyre's refugee camps requires more hope than most possess.

**Symbol and Warning:** The Mournland stands as both monument to the war's horror and warning against future conflicts. No nation wants to share Cyre's fate.

## The New Balance

**Mutual Suspicion:** Two years after Thronehold, old grievances simmer beneath diplomatic courtesy. Border disputes, trade disagreements, and cultural tensions keep ambassadors busy.

**The House Advantage:** With royal authority fragmented, the Dragonmarked Houses wield unprecedented influence. They alone can operate freely across all borders, making them invaluable and sometimes dangerous.

**Economic Interdependence:** Despite political tensions, the nations need each other. Aundair's food feeds Karrnath's cities, Breland's goods travel on Orien lightning rails, and Thrane's banks finance commerce across the continent.

**The Shadow of War:** Veterans on all sides remember the Last War's lessons. Everyone knows the peace is fragile, built on exhaustion rather than genuine reconciliation.

## Beyond the Five

**The Eldeen Reaches:** Aundair's former territory, now ruled by druids and great spirits. Their independence remains contentious.

**The Talenta Plains:** Halfling nomads who stayed neutral during the war, serving all sides as scouts and messengers.

**Zilargo:** The gnome nation whose information brokers and diplomats helped negotiate Thronehold's treaty.

**Darguun:** Former Cyran territory, now ruled by goblinoid warlords who carved out independence through military might.

**The Lhazaar Principalities:** A confederation of pirate princes ruling scattered islands, their naval power crucial during the war's final years.

**The Mror Holds:** Dwarven banking clans whose financial backing determined which armies could afford to fight another season.

**Q'barra:** A frontier territory where desperate Cyran refugees and colonial expeditions clash with ancient lizardfolk kingdoms.

**The Shadow Marches:** Orc clans and House Tharashk's dragonmarked prospectors, their bounty hunters and inquisitives serving all sides.

**Valenar:** Elven horse lords who entered the war's final phase, seeking glorious battle to honor their ancestral spirits.

**Droaam:** The monstrous realm carved from western Breland by the Daughters of Sora Kell, still unrecognized by the Treaty of Thronehold.

## For Player Characters

**National Identity:** Your character's homeland shapes their worldview, skills, and initial attitudes toward other nations.

**Cross-Border Tensions:** A Karrnathi necromancer in Thrane faces suspicion. An Aundairian wizard in Breland might be seen as arrogant. These prejudices create roleplaying opportunities.

**House Connections:** Dragonmarked Houses offer ways to transcend national boundaries, but also create conflicting loyalties.

**War Veterans:** Characters who fought in the Last War carry both skills and scars from that experience.

**The Refugee Experience:** Cyran characters face unique challenges, having lost not just their homes but their entire nation.
