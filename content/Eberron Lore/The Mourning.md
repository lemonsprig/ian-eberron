# The Mourning (20 Olarune, 994 YK)

_The day an entire nation died, and no one knows why._

## Four Minutes That Changed the World

At 11:47 AM on 20 Olarune, 994 YK, something happened in the heart of Cyre that defied explanation. Witnesses on the borders describe a wall of dead-gray mist erupting from the nation's center, racing outward at impossible speed. In approximately four minutes, this supernatural fog consumed an entire country, cities, forests, mountains, and over a million souls.

When the mist finally settled four days later, Cyre had become the Mournland: a twisted wasteland where the dead don't rest and magic itself seems broken.

## What Everyone Saw

**The Witnesses:** Soldiers, refugees, and House Orien lightning rail operators on Cyre's borders all tell similar stories—a wall of gray death advancing faster than anything natural, consuming everything in its path.

**The Silence:** House Sivis communication stones went dead simultaneously across Cyre. The last message from Metrol simply read: "The glass towers are singing. We can see the storm coming. It's beautiful and terrible and—"

**The Aftermath:** When scout parties finally penetrated the gray mists days later, they found a land transformed. Forests of glass, rivers of blood, cities twisted into impossible geometries, and everywhere—the walking dead.

## The Great Mystery

Two years later, no one knows what caused the Mourning. Theories abound, but evidence remains frustratingly scarce:

**Weapon Gone Wrong:** Some believe a superweapon backfired—perhaps a Cannith creation forge experiment, or a magical device meant to end the war decisively.

**Divine Punishment:** Thrane's Silver Flame faithful whisper that the Mourning was judgment for Cyre's sins, though they can't agree what those sins might have been.

**Aberrant Dragonmark:** A few scholars suggest an aberrant dragonmark of unprecedented power might have triggered the catastrophe.

**The Dragon Below:** Cultists of the Dragon Below claim their dark master finally stirred, breathing corruption across the land.

**Planar Breach:** Academics theorize a tear in reality itself, allowing some alien influence to poison Cyre from within.

**House Cannith Experiment:** The most popular theory blames a failed creation forge experiment in Metrol, though House Cannith vehemently denies this.

## The Cyran Diaspora

The Mourning created the largest refugee crisis in Khorvaire's history. Hundreds of thousands of Cyrans survived because they were beyond their homeland's borders—soldiers on foreign battlefields, diplomats in distant courts, merchants plying trade routes, or families who had already fled the war.

**New Cyre:** Prince Oargev ir'Wynarn, Cyre's last heir, established a refugee settlement in Breland called New Cyre. It's overcrowded, under-resourced, and filled with people who've lost everything.

**Scattered Survivors:** Most Cyran refugees live scattered across the Five Nations, often facing suspicion and discrimination. Many hide their origins, while others wear their loss like a badge of honor.

**The Unresolved Crown:** Technically, Prince Oargev still claims rulership of Cyre—or what remains of it. The other nations humor this fiction while carefully avoiding any formal recognition.

## Impact on the War

The Mourning didn't just destroy Cyre—it shattered everyone's will to fight:

**Immediate Ceasefire:** All nations pulled back from Cyran borders, too horrified to continue their campaigns.

**The Fear Factor:** If it could happen to Cyre, it could happen anywhere. No one wanted to risk triggering another Mourning.

**Technological Paranoia:** Every magical weapon, every experimental device, suddenly seemed like a potential catastrophe waiting to happen.

**The Road to Thronehold:** The Mourning made peace negotiations possible by giving everyone something worse than defeat to fear.

## Living in the Shadow

Two years after the Mourning, its effects ripple through daily life:

**Magical Anxiety:** People fear powerful magic more than ever. House Cannith faces constant suspicion about their experiments.

**Refugee Communities:** Every major city has its Little Cyre district, where refugees struggle to rebuild their lives.

**Academic Obsession:** Scholars from every nation study the Mourning, seeking answers that remain frustratingly elusive.

**Religious Revival:** Many turn to faith for explanations, swelling the ranks of the Silver Flame, Sovereign Host, and darker cults.

**Border Patrols:** The Mournland's borders are watched constantly, though no one's sure what they're watching for.

## The Questions That Haunt

**Could It Happen Again?** The fear that drives everything—if one nation could simply vanish, what's to stop it happening elsewhere?

**Who Was Responsible?** Someone or something caused this. Are they still out there? Are they planning to do it again?

**What Happened to the Dead?** A million people died in the Mourning, but the Mournland contains far more undead than there were living Cyrans. Where did they come from?

**Can It Be Reversed?** Some refugees cling to hope that Cyre might somehow be restored. Others consider such thoughts dangerous madness.

## For Player Characters

**Universal Impact:** Everyone in Khorvaire knows about the Mourning. It's the defining event of the age.

**Personal Connections:** Consider your character's relationship to this tragedy. Are you a refugee? A survivor's guilt-ridden relative? A scholar seeking answers?

**Current Attitudes:** Cyran refugees face sympathy mixed with suspicion. Some blame them for their nation's mysterious sins. Others fear getting too close to survivors of such cosmic horror.

**Adventure Hooks:** Refugees seek lost family in the Mournland. Scholars hunt for clues about the catastrophe's cause. Opportunists seek to profit from Cyran misery.

**The Great Fear:** The Mourning proved that nations can simply cease to exist. This knowledge haunts every political decision and international negotiation.
