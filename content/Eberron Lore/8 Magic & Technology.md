# Magic & Technology

_Where arcane power meets practical innovation, reshaping civilization one discovery at a time._

## The Magical Industrial Revolution

Eberron stands apart from other worlds through its seamless integration of magic and technology. Here, magic isn't the exclusive domain of tower-dwelling wizards—it's woven into the fabric of daily life, powering everything from city lights to continental transportation. This fusion created a civilization that feels both fantastical and familiar, where common folk ride lightning rails to work and send messages through speaking stones.

The Last War accelerated this transformation dramatically. Military necessity drove rapid innovation, turning theoretical research into practical applications. What emerged from a century of conflict was a world where magical technology—or "magitech"—had become as essential as food and water.

## Dragonshards: The Power Source

_"Dragonshards are to magic what coal is to steam—the fuel that makes it all possible."_

**The Three Types:** Eberron's magical revolution depends on dragonshards—crystalline formations that focus and store magical energy. Each type serves different purposes:

**Eberron Dragonshards (Blue):** Found in the earth, these power most common magical items. They fuel everburning torches, enhance weapons and armor, and provide energy for basic enchantments. Their abundance makes everyday magic possible.

**Siberys Dragonshards (Golden):** Rare fragments that fell from the dragon above, these enhance dragonmarks and create the most powerful magical items. A single golden shard might power an airship's elemental binding or focus a dragonmarked heir's abilities.

**Khyber Dragonshards (Red):** Harvested from the depths below, these dark crystals bind extraplanar creatures and fuel dangerous magic. They're essential for creating warforged, binding elementals, and powering the most ambitious magical projects.

**Economic Impact:** Dragonshard mining drives entire regional economies. House Tharashk controls much of the trade, their prospectors searching from the Eldeen Reaches to the depths of Khyber itself. A good dragonshard strike can transform a frontier settlement into a boom town overnight.

## Elemental Binding: Harnessing the Planes

_"Why use wind and flame when you can bind wind and flame itself?"_

**Living Power Sources:** Eberron's greatest magical achievement is binding elemental creatures to power large-scale applications. These aren't simple summonings—they're permanent arrangements where elementals become integral parts of mechanical systems.

**Airships:** Bound air elementals lift massive vessels through the sky. House Lyrandar's weather magic guides these floating ships along established routes, making aerial travel as routine as road travel once was.

**Lightning Rails:** Earth elementals, bound within conductor stones, channel lightning between cities at incredible speeds. The rails themselves are engineering marvels, elevated tracks that span continents and cross dangerous terrain.

**Elemental Galleons:** Water elementals propel ships faster than any wind, while fire elementals power steam-driven vessels. Naval warfare now involves capturing enemy elementals as much as sinking hulls.

**Industrial Applications:** Factories use bound elementals for heavy labor—fire elementals for forges, earth elementals for mining, air elementals for ventilation. This elemental workforce transformed manufacturing from craft guilds to mass production.

## Warforged: The Artificial People

_"We built them to fight our wars. They chose to fight for their own souls."_

**The Great Creation:** House Cannith's masterpiece, the warforged were artificial beings created for war. Built from wood, metal, and stone, then given life through elemental binding and divine magic, they represented the pinnacle of magical engineering.

**Beyond Tools:** What began as military assets became something unprecedented—artificial people with souls, dreams, and rights. The Treaty of Thronehold granted them full personhood, but social acceptance remains an ongoing struggle.

**Construction Mysteries:** The creation forges that built warforged were destroyed during the Last War, taking their secrets with them. No new warforged can be created, making each existing one irreplaceable.

**Varieties:** Different models served different roles—warforged scouts, soldiers, and specialists. Some were built for specific purposes: envoys for communication, juggernauts for heavy combat, or skirmishers for reconnaissance.

## Communication Revolution

_"Distance means nothing when thoughts travel on lightning."_

**Speaking Stones:** House Sivis developed magical communication networks that span continents. Their speaking stones allow instant conversation across thousands of miles, revolutionizing commerce, diplomacy, and personal relationships.

**Message Stations:** Major cities host message stations where citizens can send communications anywhere in the network. The cost varies by distance and urgency, making magical messaging accessible to middle-class families.

**Dragonmark Networks:** Dragonmarked house members can access more sophisticated communication methods. House Sivis encodes sensitive messages, while House Orien coordinates transportation schedules across their lightning rail network.

**Military Applications:** The Last War proved that information travels faster than armies. Nations with better communication networks could coordinate strategies, relay intelligence, and respond to threats more effectively than their enemies.

## Everyday Magic

_"Magic isn't extraordinary—it's Tuesday."_

**Urban Infrastructure:** Cities like Sharn rely on magical systems for basic services. Everburning torches light streets, bound elementals power elevators in the great towers, and magical waste disposal keeps millions of residents healthy.

**Household Conveniences:** Middle-class homes feature magical amenities that would seem wondrous to visitors from other worlds: cold boxes that preserve food, heating stones that warm rooms, and cleaning implements that work themselves.

**Transportation:** Beyond lightning rails and airships, magical transportation includes lifts in Sharn's towers, teleportation circles for urgent travel, and magical steeds that never tire.

**Security Systems:** Magical locks respond only to specific individuals, arcane alarms alert authorities to intrusion, and divination wards protect sensitive areas from scrying.

## The Dragonmarked Houses: Magical Monopolies

_"Why compete when you can control the market entirely?"_

**Exclusive Access:** Each dragonmarked house controls specific magical services through their hereditary marks. This biological monopoly makes them incredibly wealthy and politically powerful.

**House Specializations:**

- **Cannith:** Magical item creation and warforged construction
- **Deneith:** Magical security and protection services
- **Ghallanda:** Hospitality magic and food preservation
- **Jorasco:** Healing magic and medical services
- **Kundarak:** Magical banking and security vaults
- **Lyrandar:** Weather magic and elemental binding
- **Medani:** Detection magic and investigative services
- **Orien:** Transportation magic and lightning rails
- **Phiarlan:** Illusion magic and entertainment
- **Sivis:** Communication magic and message networks
- **Tharashk:** Prospecting magic and dragonshard location
- **Thuranni:** Shadow magic and assassination
- **Vadalis:** Animal handling and magical breeding

**Economic Control:** The houses' magical monopolies make them essential to modern life. They operate across national borders, their services too valuable for any government to restrict completely.

## Magical Manufacturing

_"Assembly lines powered by elemental force, producing wonders by the thousands."_

**Mass Production:** Magical manufacturing produces goods impossible through traditional craftsmanship. Identical magical items roll off production lines, making formerly rare enchantments affordable to common citizens.

**Quality Concerns:** Mass-produced magical items lack the personal touch of masterwork creations. They're reliable but not exceptional, functional but not beautiful.

**Economic Disruption:** Magical manufacturing displaced traditional craftsmen, creating social tensions between old guilds and new factories. Some regions embraced the change, while others resisted industrialization.

**Innovation Cycles:** Competition drives constant improvement. Houses invest heavily in research, seeking magical breakthroughs that will give them advantages over rivals.

## The Dark Side of Progress

_"Every convenience comes with a cost—we're only beginning to understand what we've paid."_

**Elemental Exploitation:** Binding sentient creatures as power sources raises ethical questions that most people prefer to ignore. Some elementals serve willingly, others are essentially enslaved.

**Environmental Impact:** Dragonshard mining scars landscapes and sometimes releases dangerous magical effects. The Mournland itself might result from magical experimentation gone catastrophically wrong.

**Social Displacement:** Magical automation eliminated countless jobs while creating new forms of economic inequality. Those without access to magical education or dragonmarks find themselves increasingly marginalized.

**Magical Addiction:** Some individuals become dependent on magical enhancements, unable to function without constant magical assistance.

## Regional Variations

**Aundair:** Embraces magical innovation with academic rigor. Their flying cities and weather-controlling towers represent the pinnacle of magical engineering.

**Breland:** Balances magical progress with democratic ideals. Sharn showcases both the benefits and problems of magical urbanization.

**Karrnath:** Uses magic pragmatically, including controversial applications like undead labor that other nations find disturbing.

**Thrane:** Views magic through religious lens, emphasizing purification and protection over innovation or convenience.

**The Mournland:** Serves as a cautionary tale about magical power unchecked. Whatever destroyed Cyre represents magic's potential for absolute devastation.

## The Future of Magic

**Continuing Innovation:** Research continues in fields like teleportation networks, artificial intelligence for warforged, and new forms of elemental binding.

**Ethical Evolution:** Society slowly grapples with questions of elemental rights, magical inequality, and the responsibilities that come with extraordinary power.

**Integration Challenges:** Each new magical innovation must find its place in existing social and economic structures, often causing unexpected disruptions.

## For Player Characters

**Accessibility:** Magic is common enough that most characters can access basic magical items and services, though powerful magic remains expensive.

**Career Opportunities:** The magical economy creates new professions—elemental binders, dragonshard prospectors, magitech engineers, and communication network operators.

**Social Attitudes:** Characters from different regions have varying comfort levels with magical technology. A Thranian might distrust complex enchantments, while a Brelander takes them for granted.

**Adventure Hooks:** Magitech failures create disasters, industrial espionage targets magical innovations, and ethical questions about elemental rights drive conflicts.

**House Connections:** Dragonmarked characters have access to their house's magical services, while unmarked characters might need to work around magical monopolies.
