# Eberron Essentials

_The fundamental knowledge every citizen of Khorvaire carries._

## The Eberron Calendar

**The Year:** 365 days divided into 12 months of varying lengths, following the ancient Galifaran system still used across the Five Nations.

|Month|Days|Season|Notable Events|
|---|---|---|---|
|**Zarantyr**|28|Mid Winter|The Ascension (1st) - New Year celebrations|
|**Olarune**|28|Late Winter|Bright Souls (15th) - Honor the dead|
|**Therendor**|28|Early Spring|First Hunt (5th) - Traditional hunting season opens|
|**Eyre**|28|Mid Spring|Day of Mourning (20th, 994 YK) - Memorial day|
|**Dravago**|28|Late Spring|Aureon's Crown (26th) - Academic celebrations|
|**Nymm**|28|Early Summer|Promisetide (5th) - Traditional wedding season|
|**Lharvion**|28|Mid Summer|Boldrei's Feast (17th) - Community gatherings|
|**Barrakas**|28|Late Summer|Harvest festivals throughout the month|
|**Rhaan**|28|Early Autumn|The Traveler's Curse (13th) - Day of mischief|
|**Sypheros**|28|Mid Autumn|Long Shadows (26th) - Honor ancestors|
|**Aryth**|28|Late Autumn|Thronehold Day (11th, 996 YK) - Peace celebration|
|**Vult**|29|Winter|Sun's Blessing (15th) - Winter solstice|

**Current Year:** 998 YK (Years of the Kingdom, dating from Galifar's founding)

**Weekly Cycle:** Seven days - Sul, Mol, Zol, Wir, Zor, Far, Sar (Sul and Sar are rest days in most cultures)

## ## The Thirteen Moons & Ring of Siberys

Eberron has twelve named moons plus the mysterious thirteenth moon, each associated with a dragonmark and influencing magic subtly. Arcing across the night sky like a golden bridge between worlds is the Ring of Siberys—the shattered remains of the great Dragon Above. This celestial band of golden dragonshards pulses with divine energy, its intensity waxing and waning in cycles that affect planar conjunctions and the power of dragonmarks themselves.

**The Ring of Siberys:** These gleaming fragments of the slain progenitor dragon appear as a brilliant golden arc that spans the heavens. During certain astronomical alignments, the Ring flares brighter, enhancing divination magic and making planar travel easier. Siberys dragonshards occasionally fall from the Ring—precious components that can enhance the most powerful magical items.

|Moon|Dragonmark|Associated Plane|Influence|Visibility|
|---|---|---|---|---|
|**Zarantyr**|Mark of Storm|Lamannia|Weather magic, travel|Bright, blue-white|
|**Olarune**|Mark of Sentinel|Shavarath|Protection, vigilance|Pale silver|
|**Therendor**|Mark of Healing|Irian|Healing magic, growth|Golden|
|**Eyre**|Mark of Making|Daanvi|Creation, artifice|Reddish|
|**Dravago**|Mark of Handling|Lamannia|Animal magic, nature|Green-tinged|
|**Nymm**|Mark of Hospitality|Syrania|Social magic, comfort|Warm yellow|
|**Lharvion**|Mark of Detection|Syrania|Divination, truth|Clear white|
|**Barrakas**|Mark of Finding|Lamannia|Location, hunting|Orange|
|**Rhaan**|Mark of Scribing|Syrania|Communication, records|Purple|
|**Sypheros**|Mark of Shadow|Mabar|Illusion, stealth|Dark, barely visible|
|**Aryth**|Mark of Passage|Syrania|Transportation, speed|Bright silver|
|**Vult**|Mark of Warding|Daanvi|Protection, security|Steady white|
|**The Thirteenth**|Aberrant marks|Xoriat|Chaotic magic, madness|Invisible, felt not seen|

**Manifest Zones:** When certain moons are ascendant, areas attuned to their influence experience enhanced magical effects.

## Languages of Khorvaire

Languages in Eberron are primarily regional and cultural rather than racial. A dwarf raised in Sharn speaks Common and likely Goblin, while one from the Mror Holds knows Dwarvish. Education and opportunity determine linguistic knowledge more than ancestry.

|Language|Primary Regions|Usage|Script|
|---|---|---|---|
|**Common**|All of Khorvaire|Universal trade, diplomacy, daily life|Common script|
|**Halfling**|Talenta Plains, rural communities|Ancient traditions, plains culture|Halfling script|
|**Draconic**|Academic centers, Argonnessen|Arcane studies, ancient knowledge|Draconic runes|
|**Dwarvish**|Mror Holds, mining communities|Clan business, crafting traditions|Dwarvish script|
|**Elvish**|Valenar, Aerenal expatriates|War culture, ancestral honor|Elvish script|
|**Giant**|Xen'drik, archaeological sites|Ancient ruins, giant artifacts|Giant pictographs|
|**Goblin**|Darguun, Sharn's lower levels|Regional trade, urban survival|Goblin script|
|**Orcish**|Shadow Marches, Eldeen Reaches|Druidic traditions, tribal law|Orcish script|
|**Quori**|Kalashtar communities, Sarlona|Meditation, psionic studies|Quori ideographs|
|**Celestial**|Thrane, Silver Flame temples|Religious ceremonies, divine magic|Celestial script|
|**Infernal**|Academic circles, occult studies|Forbidden knowledge, contracts|Infernal script|

**Regional Examples:**

- **Sharn Natives:** Common + Goblin (Lower City influence)
- **Mror Holds Residents:** Common + Dwarvish (regardless of race)
- **Aundair Scholars:** Common + Draconic + one regional language
- **Talenta Plains Folk:** Common + Halfling (traditional culture)
- **Darguun Traders:** Common + Goblin + one neighboring language

**Educational Languages:** Draconic (arcane studies), Celestial (divine studies), Giant (archaeology), Quori (psionics)

## Currency and Commerce

**The Galifaran Standard** (still used across all Five Nations):

|Coin|Value|Common Names|Description|
|---|---|---|---|
|**Copper Crown**|1 cp|Copper, bit|Basic coin for small purchases|
|**Silver Sovereign**|10 cp|Silver, sov|Standard coin for daily transactions|
|**Gold Galifar**|100 cp|Gold, royal|Valuable coin for major purchases|
|**Platinum Dragon**|1,000 cp|Platinum, dragon|Rare coin for extraordinary transactions|

**Alternative Currency:**

- **House Kundarak Letters of Credit:** Magical documents that transfer funds between banks
- **Trade Bars:** Standardized metal ingots used for large transactions
- **Residuum:** Magical component used as currency in arcane circles

**Regional Variations:**

- Karrnath mints coins with undead imagery
- Thrane adds Silver Flame symbols
- Breland experiments with paper currency backed by House Kundarak

## Social Structure and Titles

**Noble Houses:** Each nation maintains its own nobility, though power structures vary significantly.

**Common Titles:**

- **ir'** prefix: Lesser nobility (ir'Wynarn, ir'Gadden)
- **d'** prefix: Dragonmarked house members (d'Cannith, d'Orien)
- **Lord/Lady:** Regional authority, military rank, or courtesy title
- **Baron/Baroness:** Mid-level nobility with land holdings
- **Count/Countess:** High nobility governing provinces

**Social Classes:**

1. **Nobility:** Inherited titles and land rights
2. **Dragonmarked:** Economic aristocracy through house membership
3. **Guild Members:** Skilled tradespeople with collective bargaining power
4. **Citizens:** Free individuals with legal rights and obligations
5. **Refugees:** Displaced persons seeking new homes and opportunities

## Daily Life Essentials

**Timekeeping:**

- Hours are measured by magical chronometers in cities
- Rural areas rely on sun positions and bell towers
- Speaking stones coordinate time across long distances

**Transportation:**

- **Lightning Rail:** Magical trains connecting major cities
- **Airships:** House Lyrandar's aerial transport network
- **Orien Post:** Reliable overland courier service
- **Local:** Horses, oxen, and foot travel remain common

**Communication:**

- **Speaking Stones:** Magical communication networks
- **Sivis Message Stations:** House Sivis postal service
- **Newspapers:** The Sharn Inquisitive and regional papers
- **Heralds:** Public announcements in town squares

**Food and Drink:**

- **Staples:** Bread, cheese, preserved meats, ale
- **Regional Specialties:** Aundairian wine, Karrnathi sausage, Brelish whiskey
- **Exotic Imports:** Xen'drik spices, Sarlona teas, Frostfell delicacies
- **Goodberry Wine:** Halfling specialty from the Talenta Plains

## Cultural Customs

**Greetings:**

- **Formal:** "May the Host watch over you" (religious)
- **Casual:** "Fair winds" (traveler's blessing)
- **Business:** "Profitable ventures" (merchant greeting)
- **Military:** "Victory without dishonor" (soldier's farewell)

**Taboos:**

- Discussing Aberrant Dragonmarks openly
- Mentioning the Day of Mourning casually
- Asking about wartime allegiances directly
- Assuming someone's house affiliation

**Celebrations:**

- **Name Days:** Personal birthdays celebrated with family
- **Dragonmark Manifestation:** Coming-of-age for house members
- **Victory Days:** Regional holidays commemorating war victories
- **Ancestor Nights:** Honoring family dead during Long Shadows

## For Player Characters

**Cultural Background:** Your character's homeland shapes their knowledge of customs, languages, and social expectations.

**Economic Status:** Starting wealth and social connections depend on family background and regional opportunities.

**Calendar Awareness:** Important events, religious holidays, and seasonal changes affect adventure opportunities and NPC behavior.

**Language Skills:** Additional languages open new social circles and information sources, while ignorance can create dangerous misunderstandings.

**Social Navigation:** Understanding titles, customs, and taboos helps characters interact effectively with NPCs across all social levels.

_For detailed information about specific regional customs, house traditions, and cultural variations, see your DM's campaign notes and **Eberron: Rising from the Last War**_
