# The Last War (894-996 YK)

_A century of conflict that shattered an empire and reshaped Khorvaire forever._

## The Spark That Lit the Fire

When King Jarot of Galifar died in 894 YK, his heirs couldn't agree on succession. What should have been a peaceful transition became the bloodiest conflict in Khorvaire's history. Each of Jarot's children claimed the crown, backed by their regional power bases, and none would yield.

**The Claimants:**

- **Mishann** (Cyre) - The eldest, claimed right of primogeniture
- **Thalin** (Thrane) - Backed by the Church of the Silver Flame
- **Kaius** (Karrnath) - The military strategist
- **Wroann** (Breland) - The diplomat who tried longest for peace
- **Wrogar** (Aundair) - The ambitious youngest son

## A Century of Blood

What began as political maneuvering quickly escalated into total war. The conflict consumed three generations, with battles fought on every front:

**The Warforged Era (965+ YK):** House Cannith's creation of artificial soldiers changed everything. These tireless warriors shifted the balance repeatedly, prolonging a war that might have ended decades earlier.

**The Magical Arms Race:** Wizards weaponized magic on an unprecedented scale. Flying fortresses, elemental weapons, and arcane artillery turned battlefields into hellscapes.

**Total War:** No corner of Khorvaire was safe. Civilian populations were displaced, entire cities changed hands multiple times, and traditional rules of warfare dissolved.

## The Day of Mourning (994 YK)

The war's most horrific moment came when Cyre, the war's birthplace, was destroyed in a single day. A magical catastrophe of unknown origin transformed the entire nation into the Mournland, a twisted wasteland where magic behaves unpredictably and the dead don't rest.

**The Mystery Remains:** No one knows what caused the Mourning. Was it a weapon that misfired? Divine punishment? An experiment gone wrong? The truth died with Cyre.

**The Cyran Refugees:** Hundreds of thousands of survivors fled their homeland, becoming refugees across Khorvaire. Many still dream of reclaiming their lost nation.

## The Treaty of Thronehold (996 YK)

Cyre's destruction shocked the remaining nations into peace. The Treaty of Thronehold established:

**National Sovereignty:** Each nation recognized the others' right to exist and rule their territories.

**Warforged Rights:** The artificial soldiers were granted the rights of living beings—no longer property, but people.

**House Neutrality:** The Dragonmarked Houses were confirmed as neutral parties, operating across national borders.

**The Succession Question:** Abandoned forever. No one would claim the throne of Galifar.

## The New World Order

**The Five Nations** emerged from the empire's ashes:

- **Aundair** - Magical innovation and academic excellence
- **Breland** - Industrial power and democratic ideals
- **Karrnath** - Military tradition and necromantic pragmatism
- **Thrane** - Theocracy under the Silver Flame
- **The Mournland** - The scar where Cyre once stood

**Dragonmarked Ascendancy:** With royal authority weakened, the Houses gained unprecedented influence over trade, communication, and transportation.

**Veterans Everywhere:** A generation raised in war now seeks purpose in peace. Former soldiers serve as guards, mercenaries, and adventurers throughout Khorvaire.

## Living with the Legacy

Two years after Thronehold, the war's impact shapes daily life:

**Broken Families:** Few families weren't touched by loss. War orphans, widows, and veterans struggle to rebuild.

**Economic Upheaval:** War profiteers became wealthy while others lost everything. New trade routes bypass devastated regions.

**Technological Leap:** Military innovations found civilian applications. Lightning rails, airships, and speaking stones now connect the continent.

**Unresolved Tensions:** The treaty ended the fighting, but not the grievances. Border disputes simmer, and old rivalries endure.
