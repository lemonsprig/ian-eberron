---
title: Races & Identity
---
In Eberron, **culture matters more than race**. A character’s beliefs, loyalties, and way of life are shaped more by where they were raised and who they live among than by their ancestry. 

The aftermath of the Last War, the spread of dragonmarked houses, and centuries of migration have created a world where **racial identity is no longer a reliable marker of language, culture, or tradition**. A goblin raised in Sharn may speak only Common and know nothing of Dhakaani history, while a half-elf might grow up fully immersed in Khoravar society. Even traditional elven cultures like the Aereni and Valenar are the exception, not the rule. 

In Eberron, **your nation, background, and personal choices define you far more than your bloodline** and no assumptions should be made about a character simply because of their race.
