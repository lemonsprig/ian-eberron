## Hero Points
In a world scarred by war and shadowed by ambition, true heroes rise through grit, wit, and will. Hero Points represent moments where a character defies the odds—not by luck, but by force of spirit.

#### **Gaining Hero Points**

- Each character begins with **1 Hero Point** at the start of a session.
  
- The DM may award additional points for:
  
    - Bold or clever roleplaying
      
    - Selfless actions or personal sacrifices
      
    - Facing down danger in line with your character’s ideals or flaws
      
    - Completing key story or faction goals

_You can hold a maximum of 3 Hero Points._

#### **Spending Hero Points**
A player may spend **1 Hero Point** to do one of the following:

- **Turn the Tide:**  
    Reroll any d20 test **after seeing the result** but before knowing the outcome. You must take the second result.

- **Surge of Will:**  
    Take an **additional action** on your turn (like Action Surge). The normal rules such as only being able to cast 1 levelled spell or applying sneak attack once per turn apply.

- **Twist of Fate:**  
    Automatically stabilize when dying, or regain **1 HP and stay standing** after dropping to 0.

- **Declare an Advantage:**  
    You gain **advantage** on a test that reflects your character’s core drive (e.g. protecting an ally, unmasking a lie, striking a hated foe).

- **Narrative Declaration (DM Approval):**  
    Spend a point to assert a small narrative truth, such as:
    - “I’ve been here before.”
    - “I stashed a weapon here last time.”
    - “The guard owes me a favor.”

_No more than one Hero Point may be used per roll or round unless the DM allows it in a dramatic scene._
