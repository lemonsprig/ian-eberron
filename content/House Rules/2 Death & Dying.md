## Death and Dying
When a character is reduced to **0 hit points**, choose one of the following systems for how death and recovery work. These can be applied universally or vary by region, enemy, or dramatic moment. In all cases, a creature that is stabilized or revived gains **1 level of exhaustion** (2024 core rule).

#### **Option 1: Standard (2024 Rules)**
_Baseline D&D 2024 approach._
- Death saves are rolled as normal.
- You die after 3 failed death saves, or instantly if damage exceeds max HP.
- You gain **1 level of exhaustion** when revived or stabilized.
#### **Option 2: Wounded But Alive**
_Balanced realism—death is possible, but most wounds leave a mark._
- Use **standard death saving throws**.
- When stabilized or healed:    
	- Gain **1 level of exhaustion**, and 
    - Roll a **d20**:
		- **1–5:** Minor Injury (temporary, fades with rest or treatment)
        - **6–19:** No lasting consequence
        - **20:** Scar (lasting effect or narrative mark)

You can roll with:
- **Advantage** if healed quickly (within 1 round) 
- **Disadvantage** if felled by a critical hit or took massive damage
#### **Option 3: No Country for Old Minotaurs**
Gritty and fast-paced combat with real consequences.
- Characters **automatically fail death saving throws**. You have **3 rounds** to be stabilized or healed.
- If you take **more than 10 damage while at 0 HP**, you die instantly.
- Encourages urgency and decisive teamwork.
- Characters gain **1 level of exhaustion** when revived or stabilized.
#### **Option 4: Safety in Numbers**
_Heroic tone, focused on team survival and narrative cost._
- If a conscious **ally is within 60 feet**, you **automatically stabilize** at the start of your turn. No action required.
- A character **only dies** if:
	- **All party members** are reduced to 0 HP, or
	- They suffer **massive damage** (e.g. from a fall or critical hit while unconscious).
    - However, when reduced to 0 HP, you suffer a **Scar** (see below).
- Gain **1 level of exhaustion** when stabilized or revived.
#### **Option 5: Nothing But Scars**
_For cinematic, story-first campaigns where death is rare but scars linger._
- A character **only dies** if:
	- They choose to make a **heroic sacrifice**, or
	- It’s **narratively appropriate** (as judged by player and DM).
- Otherwise, they suffer a **Scar** (lasting mechanical or narrative wound) instead of dying.
- Gain **1 level of exhaustion**.
