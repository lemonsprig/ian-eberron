This game will primarily use the _2024 D&D 5e ruleset_. However, a number of house rules will be in place to support immersion, apply common sense, and better reflect the unique tone of the setting.

Some adjustments have also been made to accommodate the _Eberron_ campaign setting, which draws heavily from 2014 (and earlier) source material. Most notably, _half-elves_ and _half-orcs_, integral to Eberron’s lore, will remain available as player options despite their removal from the core 2024 rules.

These rules are flexible and open to discussion. The goal is to enhance everyone’s enjoyment of the game, and adjustments may be made as the campaign evolves.

## Hero Points
In a world scarred by war and shadowed by ambition, true heroes rise through grit, wit, and will. Hero Points represent moments where a character defies the odds—not by luck, but by force of spirit.
#### **Gaining Hero Points**
- Each character begins with **1 Hero Point** at the start of a session.
- The DM may award additional points for:
    - Bold or clever roleplaying
    - Selfless actions or personal sacrifices
    - Facing down danger in line with your character’s ideals or flaws
    - Completing key story or faction goals

_You can hold a maximum of 3 Hero Points._
#### **Spending Hero Points**
A player may spend **1 Hero Point** to do one of the following:
- **Turn the Tide:**  
    Reroll any d20 test **after seeing the result** but before knowing the outcome. You must take the second result.
- **Surge of Will:**  
    Take an **additional action** on your turn (like Action Surge).
- **Twist of Fate:**  
    Automatically stabilize when dying, or regain **1 HP and stay standing** after dropping to 0.
- **Declare an Advantage:**  
    You gain **advantage** on a test that reflects your character’s core drive (e.g. protecting an ally, unmasking a lie, striking a hated foe).
- **Narrative Declaration (DM Approval):**  
    Spend a point to assert a small narrative truth, such as:
    - “I’ve been here before.”
    - “I stashed a weapon here last time.”
    - “The guard owes me a favor.”

_No more than one Hero Point may be used per roll or round unless the DM allows it in a dramatic scene._

## Death and Dying
When a character is reduced to **0 hit points**, choose one of the following systems for how death and recovery work. These can be applied universally or vary by region, enemy, or dramatic moment. In all cases, a creature that is stabilized or revived gains **1 level of exhaustion** (2024 core rule).

#### **Option 1: Standard (2024 Rules)**
_Baseline D&D 2024 approach._
- Death saves are rolled as normal.
- You die after 3 failed death saves, or instantly if damage exceeds max HP.
- You gain **1 level of exhaustion** when revived or stabilized.
#### **Option 2: Wounded But Alive**
_Balanced realism—death is possible, but most wounds leave a mark._
- Use **standard death saving throws**.
- When stabilized or healed:    
	- Gain **1 level of exhaustion**, and 
    - Roll a **d20**:
		- **1–5:** Minor Injury (temporary, fades with rest or treatment)
        - **6–19:** No lasting consequence
        - **20:** Scar (lasting effect or narrative mark)

You can roll with:
- **Advantage** if healed quickly (within 1 round) 
- **Disadvantage** if felled by a critical hit or took massive damage
#### **Option 3: No Country for Old Minotaurs**
Gritty and fast-paced combat with real consequences.
- Characters **automatically fail death saving throws**. You have **3 rounds** to be stabilized or healed.
- If you take **more than 10 damage while at 0 HP**, you die instantly.
- Encourages urgency and decisive teamwork.
- Characters gain **1 level of exhaustion** when revived or stabilized.
#### **Option 4: Safety in Numbers**
_Heroic tone, focused on team survival and narrative cost._
- If a conscious **ally is within 60 feet**, you **automatically stabilize** at the start of your turn. No action required.
- A character **only dies** if:
	- **All party members** are reduced to 0 HP, or
	- They suffer **massive damage** (e.g. from a fall or critical hit while unconscious).
    - However, when reduced to 0 HP, you suffer a **Scar** (see below).
- Gain **1 level of exhaustion** when stabilized or revived.
#### **Option 5: Nothing But Scars**
_For cinematic, story-first campaigns where death is rare but scars linger._
- A character **only dies** if:
	- They choose to make a **heroic sacrifice**, or
	- It’s **narratively appropriate** (as judged by player and DM).
- Otherwise, they suffer a **Scar** (lasting mechanical or narrative wound) instead of dying.
- Gain **1 level of exhaustion**.   
## Guidance Cantrip
The _Guidance_ cantrip remains a useful tool, but to support immersion and reasonable use, the following limitations apply:
- **Declare Before the Roll**  
    _Guidance_ must be declared **before** a roll is made. It cannot be applied retroactively.
- **Short Actions Only**  
    The spell lasts **up to one minute**, so it can aid a **brief** task like climbing a rope, picking a lock, or scanning a room. It **cannot** be used for extended activities such as tracking overland, crafting, or hour-long investigations.
- **Active Awareness Required**  
    A caster must be **aware** of what the target is doing and be **actively choosing to assist**. If the caster is engaged in their own task or conversation, they cannot casually throw Guidance onto someone else.
- **Obvious Casting in Social Situations**  
    Casting _Guidance_ involves **verbal and somatic components**. While it may not emit dramatic magical effects, it is noticeable to bystanders—especially in tense or social environments. Using it in front of an NPC may affect their reaction, depending on the situation.
